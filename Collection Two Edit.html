People

Collection Two consists of a variety of different drawing styles and themes focusing on people rather than objects or landscapes. Although most of these drawings are still cartoonish, they are much more realistic and detailed than the drawings of Collection 1. One key difference is that the people in this collection have eyes and much more defined facial features. A couple common themes is the presence of a man and a woman together, James Dean styled imagery and figures, and faces that we argue are that of Charles Starkweather and Caril Fugate themselves. Starkweather was obsessed with the actor James Dean and so his drawings often depict Starkweather in a James Dean style, reminiscent of the film Rebel Without a Cause (1955). All images of the drawings, both scanned and photographs, are of materials currently housed in the University of Lincoln, Nebraska Special Archives.

Outliers

Scan of a pencil drawing. Depicts a man running or in motion, to the left of the page. The man has slicked back hair, and an outfit reminiscent of a greaser. In his right hand he his holding what appears to be a notebook. Back of the scan is blank.

The man in the drawing is with both the hair and the style of clothing is incredibly reminiscent of James Dean. This drawing is seemed to have been done free hand, as the proportions of the character are off. It is possible this is not only reminiscent of James Dean but of Starkweather himself. The character seems to be running, free to some degree as we don’t see any other image to give indication as to why he is running. And the notebook in the right hand of the man gives more insight, maybe leading the viewer to believe that the character is an artist. Not unlike Starkweather himself.
The drawing appears in the newspapre article "Starkweather Scoreboard: Seven Days of Terror." See the Articles Page.

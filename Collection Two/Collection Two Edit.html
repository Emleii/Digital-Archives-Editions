Collection Two

Collection Two consists of a variety of different drawing styles and themes focusing on people rather than objects or landscapes. Although most of these drawings are still cartoonish, they are much more realistic and detailed than the drawings of Collection 1. One key difference is that the people in this collection have eyes and much more defined facial features. A couple common themes is the presence of a man and a woman together, James Dean styled imagery and figures, and faces that we argue are that of Charles Starkweather and Caril Fugate themselves. Starkweather was obsessed with the actor James Dean and so his drawings often depict Starkweather in a James Dean style, reminiscent of the film Rebel Without a Cause (1955).

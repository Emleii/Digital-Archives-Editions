Collection One

Collection One is made up of two different types of drawing, both contain faceless figures. The majority of them are simple line drawings which mirror Starkweather’s own life: a man in jail, a woman being led guarded by a cop, a man and a woman in a car, and figures carrying guns. They seem to be drawings of Starkweather’s own memories which have astonishing psychological implications. Why are the figures faceless? Is it an attempt to dissociate from reality? To dehumanize himself, Caril, the victims, and the people around them? These more abstract drawings are the only drawings in the collection whose content links directly to Starkweather’s killing spree, although there is a drawing of a man, presumably a self portrait, holding a gun drawn in a more realistic style. It seems that Starkweather can’t bring himself to fully draw in the details of this period, fill in the lines, see the faces, and look into the eyes of himself, Caril, or the victims. Some of the figures even have eyebrows or something hinting at the shape of an eye, but never true eyes. We only have these drawings through photographs, presumably taken by or for Reinhardt when he was working with Starkweather on his autobiography. The last two drawings of the collection, however, are much more formless only displaying a vaguely humanoid figure with a head and shoulders. They are both drawn on what seems to be colored scrap paper. The originals of these are housed in the University of Lincoln, Nebraska Special Archives and the site displays scans of these originals.
